Work Experience

UNDERGRADUATE RESEARCH ASSISTANT

Intelligent Infrastructure Systems Laboratory (Purdue University), IN, May 2018 – Present

Part of the team lead by Professor Shirley Dyke aiming to develop a camera vision based deep learning algorithm to analyze structural damage caused to buildings. By using images taken of the damage caused by hurricanes, we aim to develop a program that would be able to remove the need for human presence in damage evaluation. 
My responsibilities during this time are predominantly organizing and manipulating a large database of images detailing the effects of hurricanes. By organizing the information, the computer analytics program can properly identify the desired outcome. Moreover, I feed the computer useable information using software to mark out and identify objects for the computer vision software.

ECONOMIC ANALYST INTERN

International Monetary Fund, Nairobi, Kenya, June 2017 — August 2017

As an intern within the International Monetary Fund I was required to collect and analyze data extensively to understand how the Kenyan economy reacts to elections. Reviewing official government documents and evaluating the value and accuracy of data was expected. The internship resulted in the creation of an excel document detailing the trends of the Kenyan economy quarterly from the year of 2004 to 2016, a presentation on the topic, and a written memo to be sent to the IMF headquarters. Two of the key processes of research, data acquisition and processing, are both skills that I have gained through this internship.

RESEARCH INTERN

UNITED NATIONS, Nairobi, Kenya, June 2015 — August 2015

I composed a document containing all the information that an incoming United Nations member would need to adjust to their new life in Nairobi, Kenya. This job required me to research, understand, and solve the various issues that a standard expatriate would have in their new station.
